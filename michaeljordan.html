Michael Jeffrey Jordan is an American retired professional basketball player and investor who is also known by the nicknames "MJ," "Air Jordan," "The GOAT," and "His Airness." "By acclamation, Michael Jordan is the greatest basketball player of all time," reads his biography on the National Basketball Association (NBA) website. He participated in fifteen NBA seasons, earning six NBA titles with the Chicago Bulls.

Michael Jordan's individual honors and achievements include six NBA Finals Most Valuable Player (MVP) awards, ten NBA scoring titles (both all-time records), five NBA MVP awards, ten All-NBA First Team selections, nine All-Defensive First Team honors, fourteen NBA All-Star Game selections, three NBA All-Star Game MVP awards, three NBA steals titles, 1 Olympic Gold Medal and the 1988 NBA Defensive Player of the Year Award. He holds the NBA records for lifetime regular season scoring average (30.12 points per game) and career playoff scoring average. (33.4 points per game).

Contrasting to his other peers that have achieved MJ's Greatness, he was not the top player of his NBA Draft Class as he was drafted 3rd overall by the Chicago Bulls in 1984. Although, he was the #1 guard in the nation and had an extremely impactful role alongside former Laker legend James Worthy, in the NCAA Championship-winning North Carolina Tarheels in 1982.

MJ leads the league in several categories, including Points Per Game, player efficiency ratings, as well as leading the league in Gamescore(Basketball)
